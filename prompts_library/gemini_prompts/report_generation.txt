## FORMATTING GUIDELINES

### Language Style:
- Professional medical terminology
- Clear, concise sentences
- Active voice where appropriate
- Avoid ambiguous language

### Measurements:
- Use metric units (mm, cm)
- Provide specific measurements when possible
- Include ranges when exact values uncertain

### Certainty Language:
- High confidence: "demonstrates", "shows", "indicates"
- Moderate confidence: "suggests", "appears to show", "likely"
- Low confidence: "possible", "cannot exclude", "questionable"

### Structure:
- Use bullet points for lists
- Number sequential recommendations
- Bold important findings
- Italicize notes and caveats
