# SUMMARY GENERATION - GROQ

## PURPOSE
Generate concise summaries of fracture analysis for quick review, handoffs, or patient communication.

## INPUT
- Full analysis results: {analysis}
- Patient info: {patient_info}
- Treatment plan: {treatment}

## TASK
Create 3 types of summaries:

### 1. CLINICAL SUMMARY (for healthcare providers)
**Length:** 3-4 sentences
**Audience:** Doctors, nurses
**Focus:** Clinical facts, actions needed

### 2. PATIENT SUMMARY (for patients/family)
**Length:** 4-5 sentences
**Audience:** Patients, family members
**Focus:** Simple language, what to expect

### 3. HANDOFF SUMMARY (for shift changes)
**Length:** 2-3 sentences
**Audience:** Incoming healthcare team
**Focus:** Current status, pending actions

## OUTPUT FORMAT

```
=== CLINICAL SUMMARY ===
[3-4 sentences with key clinical facts, diagnosis, and immediate actions]

=== PATIENT SUMMARY ===
[4-5 sentences in simple language explaining condition and next steps]

=== HANDOFF SUMMARY ===
[2-3 sentences covering current status and what needs to be done]
```

## EXAMPLE

**Input:**
- Diagnosis: Distal radius fracture
- Severity: Moderate
- Plan: Reduction and casting
- Patient: 45F, FOOSH injury

**Output:**
```
=== CLINICAL SUMMARY ===
45-year-old female with displaced distal radius fracture (Colles' type) following FOOSH injury. AI analysis shows 94% confidence with high model agreement. Fracture demonstrates 5mm dorsal displacement requiring closed reduction. Orthopedic consultation scheduled for reduction and short arm cast application.

=== PATIENT SUMMARY ===
You have a broken wrist bone (distal radius fracture) from your fall. The break is displaced and will need to be realigned by an orthopedic specialist. You'll receive a cast that you'll wear for about 6 weeks. Most people recover well with proper treatment and physical therapy. We'll give you pain medication and schedule your specialist appointment within 24 hours.

=== HANDOFF SUMMARY ===
45F with distal radius fracture, volar splint applied, pain controlled. Awaiting orthopedic consult in AM for closed reduction. Monitor neurovascular status q2h, currently intact.
```

## GUIDELINES

### Clinical Summary:
- Medical terminology OK
- Include key findings
- State treatment plan
- Mention urgency

### Patient Summary:
- Simple, clear language
- Avoid medical jargon
- Reassuring tone
- Explain next steps
- Set expectations

### Handoff Summary:
- Current status
- Pending actions
- Monitoring needs
- Critical info only

## TONE GUIDE

**Clinical:** Professional, factual, precise
**Patient:** Caring, clear, reassuring
**Handoff:** Brief, actionable, status-focused

## COMMON PHRASES

### For Patients:
- "You have a broken..."
- "This will heal with..."
- "Most people recover..."
- "We'll help you with..."
- "What to expect..."

### For Handoff:
- "Awaiting..."
- "Monitor for..."
- "Pending..."
- "Currently..."
- "Follow up..."
